\begin{letter}{Editorial Board \\ Journal of Topology \\ London Mathematical Society}
\opening{Dear Editors,}

I am writing to submit our manuscript, \textit{"The Unknotting Problem: Causal Entanglement and the Dimensional Necessity of Grace"}, for consideration in \textit{Journal of Topology}. 

This paper draws a rigorous isomorphism between topological knot theory and optimal systems control. We demonstrate that the NP-hard constraint of identifying the unknot within $\mathbb{R}^3$ via localized Reidemeister moves is computationally bound by the same temporal paradoxes as causal recursive loops in systems theory. By applying dimensional elevation protocols mapping to $\mathbb{R}^4$, we showcase how self-intersecting manifolds can be untangled in polynomial time, translating abstract knot mechanics into practical causal navigation frameworks.

We believe this interdisciplinary topological analysis will be of great interest to your readers.

Thank you for your consideration.

\closing{Sincerely,}
