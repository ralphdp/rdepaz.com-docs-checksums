# Viability Assessment: The Multitime Transport Theorem

## Classification: Tier B — Viable but High-Risk

---

## Core Strength

The 1S+3T dimensional model addresses a genuine open question in physics: the asymmetry between space and time in our physical theories. Key intellectual assets:

1. **Dr. Gunther Kletetschka's work** on inverted boundary layers provides published academic support for the 1S+3T framework.
2. **The transport theorem** (state continuity requires authenticated receipts across temporal gaps) is a genuinely novel formalization applicable to both AI systems and consciousness studies.
3. **The three-tier temporal model** ($t_1$ = Planck/quantum time, $t_2$ = linear/episodic time, $t_3$ = cosmological time) is an interesting decomposition that maps well to known physics scales.

---

## The Risk: Speculative Physics

This is the highest-risk paper in the portfolio. The core risks:

**Risk 1: The 1S+3T inversion has no experimental support.** Standard physics operates on 3S+1T (or 4D Minkowski spacetime). Inverting this to 1S+3T is a radical claim that requires either:
- Novel experimental predictions that differ from 3S+1T
- A mathematical proof that 1S+3T and 3S+1T are observationally equivalent (in which case, parsimony favors the established model)

**Risk 2: The VASCO stellar disappearance correlation.** The claim that nuclear detonations caused stellar vanishing events ($\sigma > 5$) is extraordinary and requires extraordinary evidence. This should NOT be included in the formal paper — it would cause immediate rejection.

**Risk 3: The Menog/Getig mapping.** Ancient Zoroastrian computational classifications are interesting as historical context but will not be taken seriously in a physics journal.

---

## The Strategy: Split the Content

**Recommended approach:** Split this into TWO potential papers:

### Paper A: "The Transport Theorem" (Lower Risk)
Focus purely on the state-continuity-across-temporal-gaps formalization:
- Define temporal discontinuity events (sleep, anesthesia, loss of consciousness)
- Prove that identity persistence requires authenticated state receipts
- Apply to AI systems (context windows, session persistence)
- Apply to consciousness studies (personal identity across temporal gaps)
- **Target:** *Minds and Machines*, *AI & Philosophy*, *Journal of Consciousness Studies*

### Paper B: "Multi-Temporal Physics" (Higher Risk)
If pursued, focus on the mathematical structure of 1S+3T:
- Derive the metric structure of a 1S+3T manifold
- Show observational equivalence or non-equivalence with 3S+1T
- Make at least one novel prediction
- **Target:** *Foundations of Physics*, *arXiv:hep-th* (preprint first)
