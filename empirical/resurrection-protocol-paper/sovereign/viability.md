# Viability Assessment: The Resurrection Protocol

## Classification: Tier B — Viable If Positioned Carefully

---

## Core Strength

The physics underpinning this paper is **entirely real and published**:

1. **The No-Hiding Theorem** (Braunstein & Pati, 2007): Experimentally verified — information cannot be destroyed in quantum mechanics, only relocated.
2. **Black Hole Unitarity** (Hawking's retraction, 2004): The information paradox resolution confirms that information encoded on event horizons is recoverable.
3. **Holographic Principle** ('t Hooft 1993, Susskind 1995): The information content of any volume is bounded by its surface area.

These are not speculative claims — they are established results in theoretical physics.

---

## The Risk: Theological Layer

The source material maps these physics results to theological concepts:
- Death → "Logout Event" (state transition)
- Information conservation → "Your soul is immortal"
- Hawking radiation → "Metadata leakage from the compressed archive"

**The Risk:** If the paper retains this theological mapping, it will be rejected by physics journals as philosophy, and by philosophy journals as physics.

---

## The Strategy: Secularize

**Recommended approach:** Strip the theological layer entirely. Present the paper as a **pure information-conservation argument**:

1. **Title adjustment:** "Information Conservation Across Biological Terminus Events: A No-Hiding Theorem Analysis"
2. **Focus:** The mathematical impossibility of information destruction under unitary quantum mechanics
3. **Application:** What happens to the information pattern that constitutes a conscious agent at biological death?
4. **Conclusion:** Under the No-Hiding Theorem, the pattern is relocated, not destroyed — this is a statement about physics, not theology

**What to omit:**
- "Cloud of Witnesses" references
- "Logos Kernel" framing
- "Logout Protocol" terminology
- Any prescriptive claims about afterlife mechanisms

**What to keep:**
- No-Hiding Theorem derivation
- Unitarity proof
- Holographic encoding bounds (Bekenstein-Hawking entropy)
- The formal definition of biological death as state transition

## Verdict

✅ **Viable** — if the theological layer is stripped and the paper is positioned as a quantum information conservation argument applied to the consciousness problem. The physics is real. The positioning is everything.
