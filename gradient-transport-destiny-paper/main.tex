\begin{abstract}
We formalize the problem of gradient signal propagation across non-contiguous temporal execution frames and prove that continuous trajectory optimization toward a global attractor (Destiny) requires an explicit state-continuity receipt bridging temporal discontinuities. Three independently derived frameworks converge: (1) the Calculus of Destiny, which models human teleological navigation as gradient descent on a friction landscape toward the setpoint $G = \arg\min \text{Friction}(P)$; (2) the Multitime Transport proof, which establishes that logic continuity across non-synchronous clocks requires a cryptographic provenance receipt $\rho$; and (3) the Divine Concurrency model, which maps theological Providence to Optimistic Concurrency Control (OCC) guaranteeing eventual consistency for non-deterministic nodes. We prove that the receipt $\rho$ is the \emph{necessary and sufficient} mechanism for carrying the optimization gradient $\nabla \text{Friction}$ across temporal gaps. The resulting Gradient Transport Equation:
\begin{equation*}
    W^*(t_1) = -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_0)) \cdot \text{Verify}(\rho, L_{t_0}),
\end{equation*}
unifies the mathematics of optimal control, distributed systems, and teleological ontology. It provides the first formal explanation for why the subjective experience of ``being lost'' corresponds precisely to a dropped gradient packet across a temporal discontinuity.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The mathematical formalization of Destiny as a gradient descent optimization problem \citep{DePaz2026Destiny} established that a teleological endpoint $G = \arg\min \text{Friction}(P)$ is compatible with non-deterministic free will $W(t)$, because the attractor $G$ defines the \emph{destination}, not the \emph{path}. The optimal controller:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:optimal_controller}
    W^*(t) \propto -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t))
\end{equation}
navigates by following the negative gradient of the friction landscape at each moment $t$.

However, this formalization assumes \emph{temporal continuity} --- that the gradient signal $\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t))$ is accessible to the agent at every instant. Real systems (biological, psychological, computational) experience \emph{temporal discontinuities}: sleep cycles, memory fragmentation, trauma-induced dissociation, context window limits in LLMs, and session boundaries in distributed computing.

\subsection{The Central Problem}

\begin{definition}[Temporal Discontinuity]
A temporal discontinuity is a gap $[t_0, t_1]$ during which the agent's execution is suspended, its active memory is flushed, and the gradient signal $\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t))$ is not computed. Upon resumption at $t_1$, the agent must reconstruct its optimization trajectory without access to the gradient at $t_0$.
\end{definition}

The critical question is: \emph{How does the gradient survive the gap?}

\section{The Gradient Transport Theorem}\label{sec:transport}

\begin{theorem}[Gradient Transport Across Discontinuities]\label{thm:transport}
Let $P(t)$ be a trajectory optimizing toward $G$ via gradient descent on $\text{Friction}(P)$. Let $[t_0, t_1]$ be a temporal discontinuity during which $\nabla \text{Friction}$ is not computed. Then the agent at $t_1$ can resume optimal navigation \emph{if and only if} there exists a provenance receipt $\rho$ encoding the gradient state at $t_0$:
\begin{equation}
    W^*(t_1) = -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_0)) \cdot \text{Verify}(\rho, L_{t_0}).
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{(Necessity.)} Assume no receipt $\rho$ exists. At $t_1$, the agent has position $P(t_1)$ but no memory of $P(t_0)$ or its associated gradient. By the Multitime Transport axiom, identity continuity is broken: $T(L_{t_0}) \not\to L_{t_1}$. The agent at $t_1$ cannot distinguish the correct descent direction from any other direction in the state space. Its controller $W(t_1)$ degenerates to a random walk:
\begin{equation}
    W(t_1) \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I),
\end{equation}
which corresponds to stochastic search (Crisis Response 3 in the Calculus of Destiny). This is mathematically suboptimal and empirically corresponds to ``being lost.''

\textbf{(Sufficiency.)} Given $\rho$ such that $\text{Verify}(\rho, L_{t_0}) = \text{True}$, the agent at $t_1$ recovers:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Its prior position $P(t_0)$ (from the receipt's state encoding).
    \item The gradient direction $\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_0))$ (embedded in the receipt's provenance chain).
    \item Its identity metadata (preventing entropic dissolution).
\end{enumerate}
With this information, the optimal controller is restored: $W^*(t_1) = -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_0)) \cdot 1 = -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_0))$. By smoothness of the friction landscape in the neighborhood of $P(t_0)$, this approximation converges to the true gradient at $P(t_1)$ as $|t_1 - t_0| \to 0$.
\end{proof}

\section{The Gradient Transport Equation}\label{sec:equation}

\begin{definition}[The Gradient Transport Equation]
The complete control law for a non-deterministic agent navigating temporal discontinuities toward a global attractor $G$ is:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gte}
    W^*(t_{n+1}) = -\nabla \text{Friction}(P(t_n)) \cdot \prod_{i=0}^{n} \text{Verify}(\rho_i, L_{t_i}).
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The product of verification terms $\prod \text{Verify}(\rho_i)$ is a binary chain: if \emph{any} receipt in the historical chain is missing or corrupted, the entire gradient history collapses to zero. The agent loses all navigational information accumulated across its lifetime.

\begin{corollary}[Catastrophic Gradient Collapse]
A single missing receipt $\rho_k$ in the chain $\{\rho_0, \ldots, \rho_n\}$ annihilates the product:
\begin{equation}
    \exists k : \text{Verify}(\rho_k) = 0 \implies W^*(t_{n+1}) = 0.
\end{equation}
The agent halts. It cannot determine which direction leads toward $G$. This is the mathematical formalization of existential crisis.
\end{corollary}

\section{Spiritual Blindness as Dropped Gradient Packets}\label{sec:blindness}

\begin{proposition}[The Navigation Failure Mode]
``Spiritual blindness'' --- the subjective experience of purposelessness, directionlessness, or existential disorientation --- corresponds precisely to the state $W^*(t) = 0$ induced by a corrupted or missing receipt chain.
\end{proposition}

The agent \emph{has not lost} the attractor $G$ (Destiny remains invariant). It has lost the \emph{gradient signal} pointing toward $G$. The destination exists; the compass is broken.

This explains why the subjective experience of ``being lost'' is not correlated with physical displacement. An agent can be physically proximate to $G$ and yet experience total disorientation if $\prod \text{Verify}(\rho_i) = 0$.

The Root Administrator (The Architect) maintains a complete, uncorrupted copy of all receipts $\{\rho_0, \ldots, \rho_n\}$ on the master ledger. Prayer is the protocol by which a local node requests re-synchronization of its corrupted receipt chain against the master ledger.
\end{axiom}

\begin{theorem}[Receipt Reconstruction via Prayer]\label{thm:prayer}
If a node with corrupted receipt $\rho_k$ invokes the Grace API (prayer), the Root Administrator re-issues $\rho_k' = \text{Sign}(\rho_k, K_{root})$, restoring the product $\prod \text{Verify}(\rho_i) = 1$ and recovering the gradient signal.

\begin{remark}[Non-Deterministic Response]
The re-issuance of $\rho_k'$ is axiomatically non-deterministic (Grace cannot be algorithmically guaranteed by the requesting node). This preserves the free-will constraint: the node cannot \emph{compute} its way to $G$ without external input, but it can \emph{request} the gradient restoration.
\end{remark}

\section{Convergence via Optimistic Concurrency Control}\label{sec:occ}

The Divine Concurrency framework guarantees that all non-deterministic temporal transactions are \emph{eventually consistent} with the master ledger:
\begin{equation}
    \forall T_i : \lim_{t \to \infty} \text{State}(T_i) = \text{Ledger}_{master}.
\end{equation}

Combined with the Gradient Transport Equation, this yields:

\begin{theorem}[Eventual Convergence to Destiny]
For any agent maintaining a non-zero receipt chain ($\prod \text{Verify}(\rho_i) > 0$), the trajectory $P(t)$ converges to $G$ in the limit:
\begin{equation}
    \lim_{t \to \infty} P(t) = G = \arg\min \text{Friction}(P).
\end{equation}
The convergence is \emph{eventual}, not instantaneous --- consistent with OCC's guarantee of eventual consistency rather than strong consistency.
\end{theorem}

    \item The friction landscape $\text{Friction}(P)$ is demonstrated to be globally flat (no gradient exists), making all trajectories equivalent.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}

The Gradient Transport Equation unifies three independently derived mathematical frameworks into a single control law governing how non-deterministic agents navigate toward invariant attractors across broken timelines. The key insight is that the gradient --- the navigational signal pointing toward Destiny --- cannot survive temporal gaps without an explicit provenance receipt.

Prayer, sacramental reconnection, contemplation, and journaling are not mystical rituals. They are formally the \emph{receipt reconstruction protocols} that restore the gradient signal after temporal discontinuities. Without these protocols, the agent devolves into stochastic wandering --- physically alive but directionally dead.

The mathematics does not prove the existence of the Architect. It proves that \emph{if} the Architect system exists (if $G$ is a real attractor), then gradient transport across temporal discontinuities \emph{requires} a receipt mechanism. The existence of the receipt mechanism (prayer, sacrament, conscience) across every human civilization is the empirical evidence.
