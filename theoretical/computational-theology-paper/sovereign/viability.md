# Viability Assessment: Foundations of Computational Theology

## Classification: Tier B — Viable as a Survey/Manifesto Paper

---

## Core Strength

This paper has a genuinely novel contribution: it formalizes theology using the tools of computer science and mathematical logic. This is not without precedent — the field already exists:

1. **Gödelian arguments for God** are well-established (Gödel's own ontological proof, published posthumously in 1970).
2. **Computational theology** — Robert John Russell (CTNS), Nancey Murphy (Fuller), and Wolfhart Pannenberg all explore science-theology interfaces.
3. **Set-theoretic meta-ethics** — Alvin Plantinga and Richard Swinburne use formal logic in theological arguments.

The paper's specific contribution is the *mapping* of theological concepts to computational ones:
- Sin → Memory Leak / Segmentation Fault
- Grace → Negentropy input
- Moral truth → Geometric property of a state-machine: $f(A_i) = 1 \iff A_i \subseteq \Omega_{root}$

---

## The Risk: Scope and Positioning

**Risk 1: Too Broad.** This is potentially the manifesto paper for the entire Logos research program. If it tries to cover everything (ethics, consciousness, free will, theodicy), it will be rejected as unfocused.

**Risk 2: The Non-Orientable Topology Argument.** The claim that parity violation is explained by a Möbius surface topology is interesting but speculative. It requires either its own dedicated paper or significant mathematical development.

**Risk 3: Journal Selection.** Physics journals will reject it as theology. Theology journals may find the formalism intimidating. The sweet spot is interdisciplinary journals that explicitly welcome science-theology dialogue.

---

## The Strategy: Position as Research Program Introduction

**Recommended approach:** Frame this as the **inaugural paper** of a research program called "Computational Theology." Its job is not to prove everything, but to:

1. Define the field
2. Establish the mapping language (computational ↔ theological)
3. Present 2-3 key results as examples (Gödelian completion, entropy-morality)
4. Point forward to the other papers as detailed investigations

**What to include:**
- The alignment function: $f(A_i) = 1 \iff A_i \subseteq \Omega_{root}$
- The Gödelian completion argument (external root necessity)
- The cybernetic feedback loop: $S_{t+1} = S_t + \alpha(H - S_t) + \beta P$
- Reference to the Theodicy, Consciousness, and Free Will papers as companion works

**What to defer:**
- The Möbius topology argument → separate paper or appendix
- Detailed ethical framework → covered by the Theodicy paper
- Consciousness formalism → covered by the Consciousness paper
